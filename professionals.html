Fonts! Fonts! Fonts!

Just as painters might use different paints to create a picture, designers use typefaces to construct their designs. Any good designer knows that different typefaces will evoke different reactions from a viewer. But who knew those reactions could be measured? Communication Arts does research to see what typefaces work best for different bits of information.

Britt Nolan Named Leo Burnett USA CCO

Britt Nolan, a longtime leader in the field of advertising and design, has recently been promoted to the position of CCO at Leo Burnett. CCO stands for Chief Creative Officer, and is responsible for the same duties a CEO would be in a non-creative firm. Read more...

The Shift from Management to Engagement

A trend has begun to surface as many design firms grow in size that shifts the focus of leadership from simply managing employees to engaging them in the work and creative process. Many companies are ditching old-school job titles such as "creative director" for more abstract titles that reflect the integration of employees rather than the hierarchy. Read more...
